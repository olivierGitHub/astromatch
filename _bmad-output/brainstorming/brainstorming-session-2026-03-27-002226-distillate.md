## Core product thesis
- Black-box algorithm: affects who appears and when; no percentage compatibility scores; avoids toxic “99% match” race while keeping astro positioning
- Differentiation: show distinctive surface signal (story, mood, timing, link type) without formula disclosure; enough to differ from Tinder; insufficient to reverse-engineer; separates marketing/identity transparency from technical transparency
- UX: narrative over number; examples: why this meetup makes sense tonight; theme of moment; moon/season; suggested relationship type; symbolic language not grades; feed = sense-making not ranking
- Risk: mystery vs frustration; visible “something” must comfort and legitimize without opening engine; design frame for what to show vs hide

## Primary signal and garnish
- Primary user-facing signal: relationship dynamic type (romantic, friendship-forward, intense, light, exploration, etc.) above plain compatible/incompatible; differentiates vs Tinder’s often fuzzy intent
- Cosmic garnish: moon, season, “climate of moment” as emotional frame and delight; supports dynamic type; not a competing score; astro present visually/emotionally without percentage

## Monetization (principles)
- Freemium: limited swipes; paid: extra swipes, super swipe, profile boost, location change; aligned with dating-app norms; do not paywall “sync window” mystically
- Tension: generic visibility boost can conflict with natural-matching promise; handle later (badges, “sponsored” transparency, etc.)
- Paid location change: market-standard; tie to algo (new sky charts/timezones) for astro coherence; narrative “your sky in Paris vs Lyon” without exposing calculation

## Trust loop (product, not algo exposure)
- Feedback path: if suggested dynamic (or feel) wrong → action like “doesn’t match me” recalibrates displayed signals/wording; does not expose or edit raw algo logic
- Distinct from Tinder-style filter tuning: app learns user’s relational language
