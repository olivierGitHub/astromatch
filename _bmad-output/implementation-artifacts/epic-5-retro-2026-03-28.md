## What went well

- **Server-side source of truth:** Quota and entitlements live on the user row / transactions, not client-only.
- **Transparent commerce:** Boost and location pass semantics avoid “guaranteed match” language.
- **Restore path:** Aligns with FR29 and support flows (ties to Epic 6 help).

## Challenges

- **Stub vs production:** Stub validation must be **disabled** when real keys are configured—document ops checklist.
- **Cross-platform IAP:** Receipt formats differ; extension points exist but full integration is workstream-sized.

## Insights

- Epic 2 entitlement behavior **finally** has a real implementation—retroactively validates the “stub until Epic 5” note.
- Billing errors should surface **actionable** messages (NFR-R1)—Epic 6 help channels complete the loop.

## Continuity — Epics 1–4

- Feed and swipe flows consume quota; mismatch remains separate from purchase pipeline per architecture.

## Preparation for Epic 6

- **Abuse-prone** surfaces (reports, blocks) should share rate-limit philosophy with auth and swipes—reuse limiter utilities.
- Help/billing links in UI should be **one tap** from billing errors—implemented in Epic 6.
