## What went well

- **Opaque feed contract:** Candidates without exploitable scoring signals supports FR14 and trust.
- **Persisted swipes (2.5):** Unlocks analytics and safety workflows in Epic 6.
- **UX components:** SessionCard, SwipeActionDock, skeleton/empty states align with UX-DR2, UX-DR5, UX-DR9–14.

## Challenges

- **Epic 2 ↔ Epic 5 coupling:** Story 2.4 requires entitlement behavior; `epics.md` notes stub or permissive paths until Epic 5—teams must keep that flag explicit in config and tests.
- **Media paths:** Feed photo access rules (viewer hasn’t swiped yet) are easy to break with a future refactor—keep integration tests.

## Insights

- **Rate limiting pattern** extended from auth to swipe bursts—same `SlidingWindowRateLimiter` pattern later used for reports/blocks.
- **Mismatch entry point** in dock foreshadowed Epic 4 without blocking Epic 2 delivery.

## Continuity — Epic 1 retro themes

- OpenAPI-first and JWT patterns from Epic 1 enabled feed routes to ship with contract checks.

## Preparation for Epic 3

- Mutual match requires **reciprocal swipe events**—data model and tests from Epic 2 are prerequisites.
- Push token (Epic 3) depends on stable **authenticated** client—no regressions in `authenticated-fetch`.
